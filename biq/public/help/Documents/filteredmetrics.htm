Filtered Metrics

When you apply a filter to a BIQ report definition, that filter will be applied to every metric included in the report. Sometimes, however, you may want to apply different filters to different metrics or even different filters to the same metric. The ability to target your filters to individual metrics comes in handy when your data repository has many different metrics with their own dimensions (i.e. attributes specific to each of them).

For example, say you once again wanted to view your company’s shipments to US Foods Company (a distributor), and also in the same report see any rebate claims that your company received for your breads and rolls products. Applying normal filters in this case will generate a result that is likely different from what you are looking for:

 

The results may look right, but think carefully. Both filters have been applied to both metrics. But what we wanted to see was the total shipments to US Foods, not just the shipments of bread and rolls products to US Foods. Similarly, we were interested in all rebate claims on bread and rolls purchases, not just the ones involving US Foods. Filtered metrics are what allow you to do this.

Create a filtered metric as follows:

1.       Click on a metric header in the report definition to expand the menu as you do to specify the sort order.

2.       Click on the funnel icon in the menu (this will only appear for metrics).

3.       A notice on the top right corner will briefly appear to inform you that you can now click on a filter value to create a filtered metric.

4.       Scroll through the filter panel to find the exact value that you would like to filter the metric by, and single-click on that value.

5.       The metric name in the report definition will now show the filter value in parentheses alongside it.

6.       After creating a filtered metric, the funnel icon will turn into a pencil. Clicking the pencil icon allows you to modify the filter value you have selected.

7.       Note: Creating a filtered metric will un-check that metric in the column panel so that you can add it again. This enables you to create multiple different filtered versions of the same base metric in a report.

Filtered metrics allow you to target applicable filters to different metrics, or create multiple filtered versions of the same metric. This is especially powerful for doing side-by-side time period comparisons (YoY, MoM, etc.), product-to-product comparisons, and similar analytical comparisons.
