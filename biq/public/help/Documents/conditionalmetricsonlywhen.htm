Conditional Metrics: Only When

When you apply a filter to a BIQ report definition, that filter will be applied to every metric included in the report. Sometimes, however, you may want to apply different filters to different metrics or even different filters to the same metric. The ability to target your filters to individual metrics comes in handy when your data repository has many different metrics with their own dimensions (i.e. attributes specific to each of them).

For example, say you wanted to view your company’s shipments to US Foods Company (a distributor), and also in the same report see any rebate claims that your company received for your breads and rolls products. Applying normal filters in this case will generate a result that is likely different from what you are looking for:

The results may look right, but think carefully. Both filters have been applied to both metrics. But what we wanted to see was the total shipments to US Foods, not just the shipments of bread and rolls products to US Foods. Similarly, we were interested in all rebate claims on bread and rolls purchases, not just the ones involving US Foods. Conditional metrics are what allow you to do this.


Conditional metrics allow you to target applicable filters to different metrics, or create multiple filtered versions of the same metric. This is especially powerful for doing side-by-side time period comparisons (YoY, MoM, etc.), product-to-product comparisons, and similar analytical comparisons.

Create a conditional metric as follows:

3.  Add a metric to your report definition and then click on the metric header.

4.  Click on ONLY WHEN (this will only appear for metrics).

5.  The Condition Metric Editor window appears. Scroll through the filter panel to find the exact value(s) that you would like to filter your metric by, and single-click on those value(s).

6.  The conditional metric name will now show the filter value in parentheses alongside it. You are able to change the default name by using the text box. To restore the default conditional metric name, simply click on the RESTORE DEFAULT NAME button.

7.  To be able to frequently re-use a conditional metric, you can save it by checking the Re-use box at the bottom of the Conditional Metric Editor window. Your saved Conditional Metrics will be visible in the Derived Metrics section that is part of the Columns panel.

8.  Click OK to confirm. 

9.   Note: Creating a conditional metric will un-check that metric in the column panel so that you can add it again.
