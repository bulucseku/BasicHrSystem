Creating and Modifying a Report Definition

Creating a report in BusinessIQ is easy; you don’t have to write any database code or understand core database concepts. If you know what your data means and how it can be sliced and diced, you’re more than halfway there. It’s easiest to first think through what you are looking for in terms of what the final results table should look like:

1.  What are the pieces of information that I want to see?

2.  How do I want to narrow down my results?

Columns are the individual pieces of information that your report will display. To select the columns that will show up in the table, click the desired checkboxes in the column panel on the left side of the screen. The column panel is divided into 2 parts: Metrics and Attributes. At least one of each has to be selected in order to produce a comprehensible report. Metrics and attributes are explained in detail in a later section. Once you click them, they will be added to the report definition:

 

The filters allow you to narrow down your results – basically deciding which rows should be included in your analysis. The available filters are displayed on the right side of the screen in a separate panel. Depending on the data type (e.g. a calendar month, text string, etc.), you will see different screen controls that are most appropriate for searching through the different values.

As an example, let’s say you’re interested in the following:

“I want to see our case volume for each product category, for just the ones we shipped to public cafeterias.”

In this example, you would specify a simple report with two columns (Cases (Metric), Category (Attribute)) and one filter (Cafeteria - Public). It would look like this after you select the columns and specify the filter:

 

Remember that only the columns that you select show up in the report results. A filter acts behind the scenes, telling the application which rows should be added up when tabulating the results and showing them to you. If you want to see the Cafeteria names in the report results, you would simply have to select the column for Customers as another column to display.

To remove columns or filters from the report definition, you can either un-check the box (in the case of columns), or you can click on the ‘X’ attached to the column or filter in the Report Definition.
