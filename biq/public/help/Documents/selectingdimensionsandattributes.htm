Selecting Dimensions and Attributes

Attributes are the second type of column, and they complement metrics in your report definition. Think of attributes as the dimensions by which you want to “slice and dice” your metrics of interest. For example, if you want to break out your sales performance by region, the “region” is the attribute and sales is the metric. Beneath the Standard Metrics and Derived Metrics in the column panel, you will see a longer list of attributes (under Attributes) that allow you to segment the data. Attributes will be grouped by Dimension. A dimension is effectively a core concept such as “Geography”, “Product”, “Time”, “Sales Hierarchy” and so on.

You cannot perform arithmetic operations on attributes, even if the value is numeric. For example, you can break out sales by ZIP code by selecting ZIP code as an attribute. However, BIQ will not let you sum or average ZIP codes in a report (this is taken care of behind the scenes when the software is configured).
